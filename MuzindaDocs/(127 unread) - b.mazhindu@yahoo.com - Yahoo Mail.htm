  • Muzinda Hub Training <training@muzindahub.co.zw>
    To:Muzinda Hub Training
    Oct 2 at 2:31 PM

    Hello developers,

    Thank you to all those who made it to the workshop on Saturday, I sure hope you learnt a lot and we going to be seeing some exciting innovative projects. By next week you would have reached the third and final part of your 2017 Digital Skills Training Program experience – The Group Development Project after your Ubuntu Business Training which you are supposed to focus on this week.

    If you did not manage to make it for the workshop, do not stress but simply follow the below guidelines on what to do and when to do it. If you are struggling to find group partners you can let me know by Wednesday the 4th of October and I will try my best to connect you guys. This is by far the most exciting aspect of the program, thus far. You have the opportunity to let your innovative and creative selves shine, by putting into action what you learnt on Treehouse.

    How does the Group Development Project work? 

    1. Form groups that are made up of 2 – 3 individuals (Feel free to choose whom you want to be in your group).
    2. The group is to come up with a creative and innovative idea for an app, website or system that will solve a problem and/or identify an opportunity within a major industry sector in Zimbabwe.
    3. There should be a clear profit motive in solving this social problem and/or identifying this opportunity.

      Major industry sectors to be considered (Select only one): Agriculture, Education, Energy, Healthcare, Media & Entertainment, Real Estate & Accommodation, Transportation, Fashion & Beauty and Religion.

    What do you need to do next?

    The groups must submit, via email, a proposal of their idea to the Training Coordinator (myself) with the following, by 12:00pm on Friday 6th October 2017.

    • The names of each member of the group.
    • Your group name.
    • The specific aspect of the development that each group member will be doing.
    • Technology used to build the project.
    • A brief overview of the idea and the problem that it is solving or opportunity it is addressing within the sector of your choice.

    These project submissions will be assessed and approved by the Training Coordinator (myself) by 5PM on Friday 6th October 2017 and the following will occur:

    • Each group will commence development of their website/application/system from Friday 6th October 2017.
    • Each group will present and demonstrate their idea to a panel of judges during the week of Monday 13th November - Friday 17th November 2017 (dates to be confirmed). 

    Remember that this group project is compulsory and you will not graduate from the program without completing it.

    As always, if you have any questions, please do not hesitate to contact me.

     

    Happy coding,

    Bernard
